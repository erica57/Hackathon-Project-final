Depression

depression

"Depression is living in a body that fights to survive with a mind that tries to die."

Depression is one of the most common mental illness that effect teenagers.This illness can be very difficult to identify especially within youth because of how their behavior is typically seen as “normal teenage behavior”.*add more info of how it affects and the dangers*

These are some signs to look for:

  • Mood Swings: anxiety, apathy, general discontent, hopelessness,and other negative emotions/feelings
  • Behavior: agitation, excessive crying, irritability, or social isolation.
  • Sleep: early awakening, excess sleepiness, insomnia, or restless social isolation
  • Whole Body: excessive hunger, fatigue, or loss of appetite
  • Cognitive: lack of concentration, slowness in activity, or thoughts of suicide
  • Weight: weight gain or weight loss
  • Also common: poor appetite or repeatedly going over thoughts

Anxiety Disorders

anxiety

"The thing about anxiety disorder is that you know that it is stupid. You know with all your heart that you're being ridiculous and you hate every minute of it."

Anxiety a mental health disorder characterized by feelings of worry, anxiety, or fear that are strong enough to interfere with one’s daily activities. Anxiety may cause people to think that they are being followed, someone is after them, or that people are judging.

  • Whole Body: fatigue, restlessness, or sweating
  • Cognitive: lack of concentration, racing thoughts, or unwanted thoughts
  • Behavioral: hypervigilance or irritability
  • Also Common: anxiety, excessive worry, fear, feeling of impending doom, insomnia, nausea, palpitations, or trembling
  • irrational thoughts

Eating Disorders

eating disorders

"All that's left will be, your bones. No flesh, no warmth, nothing to love. And still, those dreaded souls will say, 'Just not thin enough'."

There are various types of eating disorders. Some of the most known eating disorders include anorexia, bulimia, purging disorder. But some of the uncommon disorders that should not be looked over are pica and avoidant restrictive food intake disorder(ARFID)

Anorexia is a extremely popular eating disorder. This disorder causes people to obsess about weight and what they eat. This disorder is extremely dangerous; it can lead to other eating disorders such as bulimia.

Symptoms of Anorexia

  • Whole Body: dehydration, dizziness, fainting, fatigue, low blood pressure, low body temperature, osteoporosis, water-electrolyte, water-electrolyte imbalance, or feeling cold
  • Behavioral: binge eating, compulsive behavior, hyperactivity, impulsivity, or social isolation
  • Mood: anxiety, apprehension, or guilt.
  • Weight: underweight, weight loss, or extreme weight loss, and thinness
  • Development: delayed puberty or slow growth
  • Menstrual: irregular menstruation or absence of menstruation
  • Gastrointestinal: constipation or vomiting
  • Also Common: brittle nails, bruising, depression, dieting, dry hair, dry skin, headache, sensitivity to cold, or slow heart rate

Bulimia: a serious, potentially life-threatening eating disorder characterized by a cycle of bingeing and compensatory behaviors such as self-induced vomiting designed to undo or compensate for the effects of binge eating.

Physical symptoms include:

  • Often feeling cold
  • Changes in sleeping patterns
  • Menstrual irregularities
  • Dental deformities such as tooth sensitivity, enamel erosion and cavities due to constant contact with stomach acids.
  • Dry or brittle hairs and/or nails
  • Difficulty in concentration levels
  • Menstrual irregularities
  • Immune system becomes deficient causing a slower pace in wound healing.

Binge eating is a disorder in which there are recurrent episodes of eating large quantities of eating large quantities of food as defined in the DSM-5.

  • eating unusually large amounts of food in a specific amount of time, such as over a two-hour period
  • feeling that your eating behavior is out of control
  • Eating even where you’re full or not hungry
  • eating rapidly during binge episodes
  • eating until you’re uncomfortably full
  • frequently eating alone or in secret
  • feeling depressed, disgusted, ashamed, guilty or upset about your eating
  • frequently dieting, possibly without weight loss

ADHD is a chronic condition including attention difficulty, hyperactivity, and impulsiveness. This condition makes it difficult for a person to pay attention and control their impulsive behaviors. It is also important to not see ADHD a simple childhood disorder.It can continue throughout adult life and make it difficult to do basic daily tasks.

  • Behavioral: aggression, excitability, fidgeting, hyperactivity, impulsivity, irritability, lack of restraint, or persistent repetition of words or actions.
  • Cognitive: absent-kindness, difficulty focusing , forgetfulness, problem paying attention, or short attention span
  • Mood: anger, anxiety, boredom, excitement , or mood swings.
  • Also common: depression or learning disability

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD

"Having OCD is like being allergic to life-every waking moment and is spent in a state of mental hyper-sensitivity.

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder also known as OCD affects over 200,000 people per year. OCD is a common, chronic and long-lasting disorder where a person has uncontrollable, reoccurring thoughts/obsessions and compulsive behavior. This makes the person feel the need to repeat things over and over again.

  • Behavioral: compulsive behavior, agitation, compulsive hoarding, hypervigilance, impulsivity, meaningless repetition of own words, repetitive movements, ritualistic behavior, social isolation, or persistent repetition of words or actions.
  • Mood: anxiety, apprehension, guilt, or panic attack
  • Psychology: depression,fear, or repeatedly going over thoughts
  • Also common: good aversion or nightmares
